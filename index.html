DCS @Binus

A casual guide to accompany your 3-year doctoral journey.

Stage 1: Coursework

Here, you'll focus on deepening your knowledge and honing your research fundamentals.

Semester 1

You'll take four core courses as a research foundation while starting to engage in scientific discussion forums.

Required Courses (Choose a specialization):
  • CS: SMQ, KIR, RM, PoS
  • IS: RTIS, AKS, RM, PoS
Discussion Forum:
  • Colloquium 1: A forum to discuss your initial research topic.

Semester 2

Time to take one advanced course and continue your research discussions.

Elective Course:
  • IS: Advanced IT Governance (AITG)
  • CS: Advanced Soft Computing (ASC)
Discussion Forum:
  • Colloquium 2: Discussing early-stage research progress.

üèÜ GRADUATION TARGET

Second Conference Publication (Scopus-indexed)

Stage 2: Dissertation & Qualification

This is the heart of your doctoral program, where your independent research begins to take shape.

Advanced Discussion Forum

  • Colloquium 3: Discussing advanced-stage research progress.
  • Symposium 1, 2, 3: A forum to showcase your dissertation research progress.

Proposal & Qualification Exam

Prerequisite: Must complete Colloquium 1, 2, and 3.

  • Research Proposal Exam: You must present and defend your research proposal before the examiners.
  • Qualification Exam: A comprehensive exam to test your understanding. Passing this officially makes you a Doctor Candidate (Cand. Dr.).

Stage 3: Graduation (Final Exams & Ceremony)

The final phase before you earn your Doctorate degree. You've got this!

Final Presentation & Key Publication

  • Research Result & Dissertation Symposium: The final presentation moment for all your research and dissertation results.

üèÜ GRADUATION TARGET

Second Journal Publication (Scopus-indexed)

Final Exams

  • Closed Exam: A dissertation defense in front of an internal board of examiners.
  • Open Exam: The final defense of your dissertation before a wider audience, including external examiners.
